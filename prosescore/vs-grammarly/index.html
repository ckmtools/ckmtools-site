8 readability formulas in one tool

Grammarly uses a single proprietary readability score. ProseScore runs all eight major readability algorithms and averages them into a consensus grade for a more accurate, well-rounded assessment.

Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level

The most widely used readability formula. Maps text to a U.S. school grade level based on sentence length and syllable count.

Gunning Fog Index

Estimates the years of formal education needed. Penalizes complex (3+ syllable) words more heavily than Flesch-Kincaid.

Coleman-Liau Index

Uses character count instead of syllables, making it more reliable for technical text with acronyms and abbreviations.

SMOG Index

Considered the gold standard for health literacy. Counts polysyllabic words to estimate reading difficulty.

Automated Readability Index

Character-based formula designed for automated assessment. Fast and effective for large documents.

Dale-Chall Readability

Compares words against a list of 3,000 familiar words. Particularly accurate for lower reading levels.

Spache Readability

Designed specifically for primary-grade text (grades 1–3). Uses a familiar word list tuned for early readers.

Flesch Reading Ease

Scores text on a 0–100 scale (higher = easier). The most intuitive readability metric for non-technical users.
